I'm currently attending Carleton University as a graduate student in the Applied Mathematics stream. My research focuses on "recursions", functions whose output depends on preceding terms in a given sequence, and how we can visually understand them and their complex structures.

In the furture, I hope to apply and challenge myself obtain a position as a cryptographer -- someone who practices and studies techniques for secure communication in the presence of third parties.

As a mathematician, I've recently enjoyed taking part in competitions. I currently tackle challenges found at Project Euler and post my solutions on my GithHub account. To view those solutions and all my programming projects I've developed, click on the GitHub icon below.

I'll also be starting some YouTube videos on some topics in mathematics that support the research I'm doing at Carleton University.

Finally, I like to play piano and learn languages. I'm currently learning French and Ukrainian. Furthermore, in November, I plan to pick up Polish. You can follow me on my Duolingo account by clicking on the big, green owl below!
